A 62-year-old woman with systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE) was evaluated for joint pain and rash. Physical examination revealed a malar rash and swelling in the small joints of the hands. Laboratory tests showed elevated anti-dsDNA and low complement levels. A skin biopsy confirmed lupus involvement.

A 45-year-old man with type 2 diabetes mellitus was seen for evaluation of foot ulcers. Examination showed a deep ulcer on the plantar surface of the left foot with surrounding erythema. Ankle-brachial index was low, indicating peripheral arterial disease. Culture of the ulcer grew Staphylococcus aureus.

A 70-year-old woman with chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) was admitted for worsening dyspnea and wheezing. Chest X-ray revealed hyperinflation and flattened diaphragms. Pulmonary function tests showed a decreased FEV1/FVC ratio. The patient was started on bronchodilators and steroids.

A 29-year-old man with Crohn's disease presented with abdominal pain and diarrhea. CT of the abdomen showed thickening of the terminal ileum and mesenteric lymphadenopathy. Colonoscopy confirmed active ileitis. The patient was started on biologic therapy.

A 50-year-old woman with rheumatoid arthritis was evaluated for morning stiffness and joint swelling. Physical examination revealed synovitis in the wrists and MCP joints. Laboratory tests were positive for rheumatoid factor and anti-CCP antibodies. The patient's DMARD therapy was adjusted.

A 65-year-old man with atrial fibrillation presented with palpitations and fatigue. ECG showed atrial fibrillation with a rapid ventricular response. The patient was anticoagulated with warfarin and underwent electrical cardioversion, which successfully restored sinus rhythm.

A 72-year-old woman with congestive heart failure was admitted for evaluation of shortness of breath and leg edema. Echocardiography revealed reduced ejection fraction and dilated cardiomyopathy. Diuretics were initiated, and the patient was advised on dietary sodium restriction.

A 55-year-old man with chronic kidney disease was seen for evaluation of worsening anemia. Laboratory tests showed a hemoglobin level of 9.5 g/dL and elevated serum creatinine. The patient was started on erythropoiesis-stimulating agents and referred for further nephrology evaluation.

A 38-year-old woman with hypothyroidism presented with fatigue and weight gain. Laboratory tests revealed elevated TSH and low free T4 levels. The patient's levothyroxine dose was adjusted, and she was counseled on the importance of medication adherence.

A 47-year-old man with hepatitis C infection was evaluated for elevated liver enzymes. Ultrasound of the abdomen showed hepatomegaly and mild steatosis. The patient was started on direct-acting antiviral therapy and counseled on avoiding alcohol and maintaining a healthy diet.
